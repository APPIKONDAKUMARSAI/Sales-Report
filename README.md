# Sales-Report
1. Strong overall profitability

Total Sales: 55.4M vs Profit: 31.6M → ~57% profit margin
Indicates high-margin product mix and effective pricing strategy

2. Revenue concentrated in few categories

Computers (21.3M) and Cameras & Camcorders (17.0M) contribute ~69% of total sales
Heavy dependence on top 2 categories → potential risk if demand shifts

3. High-performing subcategories drive profit

Camcorders (21.4%) is the top profit contributor
Followed by Projectors & Screens (15.7%) and Laptops (13.1%)
Profit distribution is skewed toward specific premium products

4. Geographic profit concentration

United States and China dominate profits, with a large gap vs other countries
Indicates strong market penetration but also regional dependency

5. City-level performance variation

Cities like Beijing, Seattle, Paris, and Shanghai generate highest profits
Suggests urban, high-income markets are primary revenue drivers

6. Regional imbalance

North America leads, followed by Asia and Europe
Opportunity exists to scale performance in underperforming regions

7. Manufacturer contribution disparity

Few manufacturers contribute disproportionately to profit
Suggests reliance on key vendors → supply chain risk if disrupted

8. High volume operations

15K orders and 251K units sold
Indicates strong demand and operational scalability

9. Cost efficiency

Cost of Sales: 23.8M vs Sales: 55.4M
Healthy cost structure supporting high profitability

10. Long-tail categories underperform

Categories like Audio and Movies/Books contribute <5% each
Candidates for optimization or discontinuation
